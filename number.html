Country Wise Arrivals
Canada and the USA: Both Canada and the USA exhibited consistent growth in the number of travelers visiting India throughout the period. However, there was a drastic decline in arrivals in 2020 due to the COVID-19 pandemic, with Canada experiencing a decrease of approximately 65% and the USA witnessing a decline of around 74% compared to the previous year.

Argentina and Brazil: Argentina showed a fluctuating trend in visitor arrivals to India, with a significant drop of around 67% between 2019 and 2020. Similarly, Brazil also experienced a decline of approximately 68% in 2020 compared to the previous year.

European Countries: Several European countries, such as Austria, Belgium, Denmark, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Ireland, Italy, Netherlands, Norway, Portugal, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, and the United Kingdom, had a consistent increase in traveler arrivals to India during the analyzed period. However, like other regions, there was a considerable decline in arrivals in 2020, ranging from 27% to 77%.

Russia and Ukraine: Russia exhibited significant fluctuations in traveler arrivals, reaching its peak in 2017 and experiencing a decline of around 61% in 2020. Ukraine followed a similar trend, with a decline of approximately 64% in 2020 compared to 2019.

Asian Countries: Among the Asian countries, Bangladesh and Nepal stood out with substantial increases in traveler arrivals to India. Bangladesh witnessed a remarkable growth of around 481% from 2014 to 2019, while Nepal experienced a consistent upward trend with a peak in 2018. However, both countries experienced a decline in 2020, with Bangladesh showing a decrease of approximately 53% and Nepal witnessing a decline of around 36%.



Foreign Exchange Earning

From 2014 to 2015, there was an increase of 3.6% in foreign exchange earnings.
From 2015 to 2016, there was a further increase of 3.7%.
The highest year-on-year increase occurred from 2016 to 2017, with a significant jump of 14.0% in foreign exchange earnings.
The growth rate slowed down from 2017 to 2018, with a decrease of 18.6% in foreign exchange earnings.
However, there was a recovery in 2019, with an increase of 10.1% compared to the previous year.
The year 2020 experienced a substantial decline of 69.1% in foreign exchange earnings due to the COVID-19 pandemic.

From January to March, there was a gradual decrease in earnings in 2014, followed by a slight increase in April.
In 2015, earnings showed a consistent increase from January to June, with July being the highest-earning month.
In 2016, there was a steady increase in earnings from January to June, with June recording the highest earnings.
In 2017, there was a general upward trend from January to November, with December marking the highest-earning month.
In 2018, there was a significant drop in earnings from April to December.
2019 saw a consistent increase from January to March, followed by a gradual decline until October, with November being the highest-earning month.
In 2020, there was a steep decline in earnings, particularly from March to May, followed by a gradual recovery from June to December.
